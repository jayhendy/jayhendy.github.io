About


I'm a 3rd Year PhD Student in the Human-Computer Interaction Lab at the University of Waterloo, advised by Dr. Edward Lank. My research explores the design, development and assessment of gesture-based interaction systems, with particular focus on facilitating learning and supporting ubiquitous scenarios. I leverage techniques in human computer interaction, machine learning, cognitive science and psychological theory.

Prior to Waterloo, I completed my undergraduate degree at Mount Allison University, with an honours thesis in assessing the usability of course registration systems and double minors, in Mathematics and Psychology.
