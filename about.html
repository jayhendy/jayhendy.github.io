About


I'm a 3rd Year PhD Student in the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Lab at the University of Waterloo, advised by Dr. Edward Lank. My research explores the design, development and assessment of gesture-based interaction systems, with particular focus on facilitating learning and supporting ubiquitous scenarios. I leverage techniques from human computer interaction, machine learning, cognitive science and psychological theory. In 2018 I held a research internship at Huawei Technologies Canada, in the Noah's Ark Lab HCI division.

Prior to Waterloo, I completed my undergraduate degree at Mount Allison University, with an honours thesis in the usability of course registration systems and double minors, in Mathematics and Psychology.
